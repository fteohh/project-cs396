What are yuru-chara (ゆるチャラ)?

⌄

Yuru-chara are a type of Japanese mascot character that promote a place, region, event, organization, or business.

They are often created by local governments and other organizations to stimulate tourism and economic development by creating an identity.

You can meet yuru-chara at events, festivals and meet-and-greets, as well as view them on TV programs!


The term was first created by Jun Miura in the early 2000's.

The name yuru-chara is a contraction of yurui mascot character (ゆるいマスコットキャラクター). In general, the adjective yurui (緩い) means "loose", but also has other connotations including "gentle", "weak", "laid-back", "light-hearted" or "unimportant".

According to Miura, a yuru-chara must meet three requirements:

1. It must convey a strong message of love for one's hometown or local region
2. The character's movements/behavior should be unique and unstable or awkward
3. The character should be unsophisticated, laid-back, and lovable

yuru-chara map employs an interactive map that allows users to “explore” Japan and learn more about various regions, prefectures, and cities through their yuru-chara.

Yuru-chara often incorporate motifs that represent local culture, history, legends, wildlife, architecture, geography, or produce. This is often portrayed in their physical appearance in an amusing way.

Their name are often a play on words, such as with Kumamon (from Kumamoto Prefecture).
